   HANDHELD SIMULATION LOGIC:

   1. GHOSTING & MOTION BLUR: 
      Old handhelds (DMG/Game Gear) had slow pixel response. To simulate 
      this, use a "Frame Blending" shader (Response Time > 60ms).

   2. COLOR CORRECTION (Saturation Boost):
      Original GBA and GBC screens lacked a backlight (No B/L). Developers 
      oversaturated colors to compensate. Set SATURATION to 1.4 for GBA 
      to match the intended look on modern bright screens.

   3. THE GREEN TINT (DMG-01):
      For the original GameBoy, use a palette shader with:
      R: 0.54, G: 0.61, B: 0.06 (Darkest)
      R: 0.13, G: 0.22, B: 0.13 (Lightest)

   4. PIXEL GRID VS SCANLINES:
      Handhelds use a "Grid" mask (tiny gaps between pixels) rather than 
      horizontal scanlines. Set your mask intensity to 0.3 for realism.

   5. SIGNAL NOISE:
      Handhelds have zero RF noise. Set sig_noise to 0.0 unless simulating 
      a "Game Boy Player" on a CRT TV.
